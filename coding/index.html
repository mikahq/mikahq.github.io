Agent Based Modeling

Agent based modeling is a computer simulated environment in which agents interact with other agents within an environment defined by manipuable parameters. This example delves into the schelling-segregation-model, where the purpose was to determine whether segregation can be explained by individual motivations to avoid being a local minority. To execute this, agents will move around a two-dimensional space depending on whether they are a racial minority within their local neighborhood.

Detailed code, results and methodology can be found here.

I use R not only for complex statistical analyses but also to engage in hypothesis testing for my research. Writing in code makes generalizations and assumptions much more explicit. Further R scripts of other ABMs can be found here. This example looks at assortative mating choice markets.
