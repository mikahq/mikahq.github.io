Children’s Judgments of Dual Identities

Collected data and analyzed children's judgments of biracial individuals.

Presented poster at URCA conference.

Contributed to the resulting white paper on child social development.

Overview

The U.S. is increasingly diverse, and people are given the option to claim more than one racial identity.

In this research study I investigated how children view biracial individuals who flexibly present their identity based on context or by the situation.

It is important to understand how people perceive group membership (e.g., can I trust this person? Are they an in-group or out-group member?). One way of studying this is through a developmental lens.

Past research suggests:

  • Adults judge biracial individuals who change how they racially identity based on context, known as Contextual Racial Presentation (CRP) negatively.
  • Children use social markers such as race as a cue for ingroup (like me) and outgroup (not like me) for deciding who they associate with.
  • Biracial people present an interesting conundrum because they have membership in multiple social groups. 

Poster Presentation

As children got older they were less likely to say it was OK when the dual-identity person claimed a monoracial identity.

Next steps...

Methodological Improvements
  • Include diverse racial/ethnic groups in sampling
  • Explore judgments of both high-status and lower-status monoracial children
  • Control for and investigate the impact of perceptual features on racial categorization

Deeper Investigative Directions

  • Examine the role of mutual intentionality in group membership
  • Study how group approval might influence perceptions of CRP
  • Investigate how the stability of a participant's own racial identity affects CRP judgments

Methodology

I used a mix of the following research methods to gather data:

  • Participants: 303 6-13- year-olds collected from Online and Offsite testing at a local zoo.
  • Method: 2 x 2 between-subjects design, became 2 condition design (main character either maintains their biracial identity or switches to a monoracial identity.
  • Conducted logistic and linear regressions, Chi-squared test, Welch’s two-sample t-test to run pre-registered analyses and explore the data.
