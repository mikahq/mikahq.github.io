Visualizing libraries, books, and their patrons

Summertime and the (reading) is easy

The San Francisco Library boasts multiple branches in a densely populated city. They have an impressive dataset spanning 2004-2016, so I took a look.

While readership varies in other months, June, July and August see a massive spike in readership, regardless of the branch location.

Generational differences in library communications.

The preference for contact methods in library communications underwent a shift around 2013. Phone, print and email were neck in neck until email rose ahead drastically.

While the majority of people checking out books skewed older, around 65+ years, they had a more even preference for all methods. This constrasts with patrons under 55 years, who much prefer email.

Interrogating The New York Times reading list

The New York Times came out with a list of the "best books of the 20th century" polling different famous authors. Among them was Stephen King (a personal favorite). Here, I was tasked to draw out differences and make comparisons.

We can see that out of the 100 books in the list, 71 of them were fiction.

Non-fiction was a scarcer genre. Yet when we look at ratings from the general public, non-fiction consistently ranked more highly than fiction, above a 4.0/5.0.

Secondly, I created an organized category of sub-genres by main genre. Historical books had a good mix of both fiction and non-fiction, but the genres were mainly siloed. Why aren't books, at least in this list, doing genre-cross overs? What about genres not mentioned or captured in this list?

Scope

Choosing the message of the visual is key

  • Before drafting a viusalization, writing down the goal or takeaway is crucial to guide the format I decide to go with!
Understanding information will be left out
  • When I choose to represent one kind of information or transform the data, there will always be other types of information I may omit for clarity.
Always room for improvement
  • Not all of the visualizations I create will be as clear as possible, but I strive to incorporate feedback in the re-design!
